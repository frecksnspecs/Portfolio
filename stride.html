Idea

Background

Most communal venues subject you to listen to music that was picked by someone with mediocre taste (at best), and if you've ever had to curate a playlist to satisfy your friends' mixed tastes, you know just how daunting the task can be.

The goal for our team of developers and designers was to build an app in 24 hours that put more power in the hands of listeners.

Goals

  • Flexible system
  • Fast engagement from listeners

Roles

  • UI Designer
  • UX Designer

Research

Competition

There were 2 other apps in the App Store that offered similar services, one came packaged with the hardware to host a music library and the other was seen as a premium app and users arenâ€™t allowed to request new music.

Conclusion: Source music through Spotify and remove the need to host music locally. Listeners’ site is a free, no sign-up web app that communicates with host app.

Context

We talked to local businesses (University Union, Coffee Shops, Gyms, College Radio) and found that most were using Pandora or their own iPod to play music. Students expressed the most interest in the app and saw it as a way to veto “Country Wednesdays.”

Conclusion: App would need to be free for personal use to compete with Pandora, but a business would need to purchase a subscription to the service. In the process, also gathered how the app should function and piqued the interest of local businesses to become future subscribers.

Result

Democracy Accomplished

Only the person playing the music using Juqbox is required to download the Player app. Listeners simply go on the host’s web app address via URL or QR code to vote on or request new music.

In 24 hours, our team’s talented developers were able to create a build of the app that could be demo’d in front of a live audience.
